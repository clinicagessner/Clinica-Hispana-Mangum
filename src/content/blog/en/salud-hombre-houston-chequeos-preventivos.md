Many men only see a doctor when something hurts. The problem is that high blood pressure, diabetes, and high cholesterol give no warning: they can progress for years without symptoms. A timely preventive checkup catches these problems while they still have a simple solution.

## Why should men get regular checkups?

In Houston's Hispanic community it's common to push through discomfort "until it passes" because of work, lack of insurance, or the language barrier. But the conditions that affect men the most —heart disease, type 2 diabetes, high blood pressure— are far easier to control when caught early. An annual checkup takes less than an hour and gives you a clear map of your health.

## Conditions we treat frequently

- **High blood pressure:** the "silent disease" that damages the heart and kidneys without symptoms.
- **Type 2 diabetes:** very common in our community; early detection prevents complications.
- **High cholesterol and triglycerides:** they raise the risk of heart attack and stroke.
- **[Urinary tract infections](/en/services/infecciones-urinarias):** burning or discomfort when urinating that you shouldn't ignore.

## Recommended exams by age

| Age | Recommended checkups |
| --- | --- |
| 18–39 | Blood pressure, glucose, cholesterol, weight and BMI; STI testing if sexually active |
| 40–49 | All of the above + electrocardiogram, prostate review based on family history, and hormone panel if symptoms appear |
| 50–64 | All of the above + prostate test (PSA) and colon cancer screening as medically indicated |
| 65+ | Full annual checkup, up-to-date vaccines, and vision, hearing, and memory evaluation |

Everyone is different: at your visit, the doctor adjusts these exams to your history and risk factors.

## Warning signs: don't ignore them

Seek care as soon as possible if you have:

1. Chest pain or pressure
2. Shortness of breath during activities you used to tolerate
3. Excessive thirst and frequent urination
4. Blood in your urine or stool
5. Pain, a lump, or changes in your testicles
6. Intense, frequent headaches
7. Extreme fatigue or unexplained weight loss

## Practical tips to protect your health

- Get a general checkup **once a year**, even if you feel fine.
- Walk at least 30 minutes a day and cut back on sodas and sugary drinks.
- Know your numbers: blood pressure, glucose, and cholesterol.
- Don't stop medications on your own; ask your doctor first.
- Sleep 7–8 hours: rest is part of your health too.
